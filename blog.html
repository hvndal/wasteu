Maynard's New Residential Waste Program: What It Means for You

Those massive spring cleanouts, old couches, and stacks of broken down Amazon boxes from your latest home project are no longer going to be magically whisked away on Tuesday mornings.

Our residential roll-offs are the exact answer to the town's new limits. You don't need to break down boxes, chop up furniture, or worry about municipal limits. You throw it in the bin, and we handle the rest.

We're running a localized push to help Maynard residents clear out their garages and basements without dealing with the town bureaucracy. Skip the municipal headache.

ON THE ROAD By Connor (Operations) June 28, 2026

Navigating the Hills of Harvard: A Masterclass in Dumpster Drop-offs

Anyone who has driven through Harvard, MA knows it's absolutely gorgeous. It's also an operational nightmare for driving a massive, heavy-duty commercial truck carrying a steel box filled with several tons of construction debris.

Last week, we got a call for a 20-yard roll-off drop for a massive home renovation deep in the woods of Harvard. The driveway was essentially a 30-degree incline composed of loose gravel, winding through 100-year-old oak trees with barely an inch of clearance on either side.

The national waste chains wouldn't touch it. They told the homeowner they'd have to drop the dumpster at the bottom of the hill, right on the edge of the busy state route—which is a massive safety hazard and requires the poor contractors to haul heavy debris a quarter-mile down a hill.

When Damien and I rolled up, we didn't just back in blindly. It took precise spotting, locking the differentials on the truck, and creeping back at half a mile an hour. We utilized heavy-duty blocking to ensure the dumpster wouldn't slide, and we managed to place it exactly three feet from the contractor's demo window at the top of the hill.

That is the difference between a massive faceless corporation and a local crew that actually knows how to drive in New England. If you've got a tricky driveway, don't let the big guys tell you it can't be done.
